The first time I used Linear, I thought something was broken. It was just... fast. Genuinely, surprisingly fast.

## What They Got Right

Linear made an explicit bet that **speed is a feature**, not a nice-to-have. Their desktop app feels native. Keyboard shortcuts work everywhere. The issue creation flow takes seconds.

They also made strong opinionated choices: no custom fields, no endless configuration, no "enterprise flexibility." That takes courage when every competitor is adding more.

## The Design Philosophy

What I find most interesting is that Linear treats product management like a craft. They write publicly about their decisions. They're not trying to be everything to everyone — they're trying to be exactly right for the people they care about.

## What I'd Push On

The reporting side is still underdeveloped. If you're a manager trying to present progress to non-engineers, you're mostly exporting to spreadsheets. There's an opportunity to make cycle data beautiful and communicable without losing the developer-first feel.

## Why I'd Want to Work There

Small team, high craft, strong opinions. The kind of place where what you ship actually matters.
